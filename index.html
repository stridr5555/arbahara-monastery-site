Monastery of Abuna Hara Dengeel • Dallas, Texas

Modern Ethiopian sanctuary designed with light, calm, and devotion

Arbahara is planning a luminous campus with glass volumes, wide linen courtyards, and dark orange accents rooted in Ethiopian Orthodox traditions. Our Dallas build combines prayer, education, and diaspora stewardship.

Information is also available at arbahara.info (P.O. Box 850721, Richardson, TX).

Vision & Sanctuary

The Dallas campus centers on prayer, hospitality, and culture. There will be spacious hallways for communal cooking, light-filled iconography, and a listing of monthly planning sessions for supporters from the diaspora.

Spaces of stillness

The nave will integrate halo lighting, stone floors, and seating tuned to Zema chanting so families feel grounded in the rituals.

Community pulse

Circles gather for prayer, crafts, and stewardship. Each update is shared via the mailing list in the P.O. Box notice and arbahara.info.

Sanctuary services

Weekly liturgies, youth education, cultural programs, and diaspora fundraisers keep the project advancing through 2030 and beyond.

Planning Timeline

These are the planning phases we are tracking for the next five years.

  1. 2029 • Planning & Site Development

    Finalize land acquisition, engineering studies, and zoning approvals while inviting design partners to share proposals.

  2. 2030 • Design & Community Workshops

    Detail the sanctuary layout, gather feedback during monthly Dallas liaison meetings, and align fundraising milestones.

  3. 2031 • Construction & Cultural Programming

    Break ground on the main nave, install lighting, and schedule cultural residencies ahead of the grand opening planning.
