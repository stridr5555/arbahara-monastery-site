Monastery of Abuna Hara Dengeel • Dallas, Texas

Modern Ethiopian sanctuary, built with light, color, and community devotion

A serene campus of cream linen backdrops, luminous glass volumes, and dark orange accents anchored in Ethiopian Orthodox heritage. Arbahara brings diaspora members together through prayer, education, and sanctuary hospitality.

Vision & Sanctuary

Arbahara is crafting a light-filled sanctuary that blends contemporary materials with the rhythms of the Ethiopian Orthodox Church. Expect luminous glass panels, cream linen textures, and bespoke iconography that reflects the legacy of Abuna Hara.

Spaces of stillness

The nave features a floating halo ceiling, stone floors warmed by dark orange lighting, and a seating plan that invites diaspora families to experience the Divine Liturgy together.

Community pulse

Membership circles gather weekly for prayer, storytelling, and stewardship. Each tier receives WhatsApp notifications, Twilio reminders, and curated experiences for patrons.

Sanctuary services

Weekly liturgies, youth education, cultural programs, and diaspora fundraising events provide the lift for this project to scale.

Build Timeline

Read the milestone cards below for the phases we are executing with Dallas artisans.

  1. 2025 • Foundation & Pilgrimage

    Structural core, stonework, and a welcoming pilgrim hall with cream terrazzo and etched iconography.

  2. 2025 Q4 • Sanctum Assembly

    Nave completion, halo lighting, iconostasis planning, and acoustic tuning for Zema chants.

  3. 2026 • Community Launch

    Interior finishes, membership halls, cultural programming, and the formal Dallas opening.
